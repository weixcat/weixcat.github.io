data basic

Posted on 2018-04-05 | In Statistics

week 1

data matrix

 row: observation/case
 column: variable

type of variables

 numerical: (quantitative) sensible to do arithmetic operations
  continuous & discrete
 categorical: (qualitative) limited number…such as gender
  ordinal(有顺序，程度) & (regular)categorical

relationships between variables

 associated or dependent: positive & negative
 independent

studies

observational: observe(不改动数据), only establish an association
  retrospective: uses past data
  prospective: data are collected throughout the study
experimental: randomly assign subjects to treatments, establish causal connections

confounding variables

extraneous(无关) variables that affect both the explanatory and the response variable, and that make it seem like there is a relationship between them.

correlation does not imply causation.

sources of sampling bias

convenience sample & non-response & voluntary response

sampling method

simple random sample(SRS) stratified sample(分层抽样) cluster sample multistage sample

principle of experimental design
  1. control 2. randomize 3. replicate 4. block

placebo for control group, which is opposite for treatment group.
blind & double-blind(对比试验)

week 2

Generally, in a scatter plot, we place the explanatory variable on the x axis and the response variable on the y axis.

evaluating the relationship

direction: positive & negative
shape: linear & curved
strength: strong & weak
outliers(边界值)

visualizing numerical data

histogram(直方图):
provides a view of the data density
especially useful for describing the shape of the distribution

dotplot:
useful when individual values are of interest
can get busy as the sample size increases

box plot:
useful for highlighting outliers, median(中位数), IQR(四分位距)

map density:
useful for highlighting the spatial(空间) distribution

key characteristic

skewness:
left skewed, symmetric, and right skewed
(the direction of the tail)

modality:
unimodal, bimodal, uniform, and multimodal
(classified by the number of peak)

measures of center:
mean, median, mode(众数)

measures of spread:
range: max - min
variance
standard deviation
inter-quartile range: Q3 - Q2

robust statistics

definition: measures on which extreme observations have little effect
such as: median IQR

transforming data

1.a transformation is a rescaling of the data using a function
2.when data are very strongly skewed, we sometimes transform them so they are easier to model

(natural) log transformation:
1.often applied when much of the data cluster near zero (relative to the larger values in the data set) and all observations are positive
2.to make the relationship between the variables more linear,and hence easier to model with simple methods

other transformation: square root, inverse

goals of transformations:
1.to see the data structure differently
2.to reduce skew assist in modeling
3.to straighten a nonlinear relationship in a scatterplot

exploring categorical variables

frequency table & bar plot
contingency table
segmented bar plot:
1.useful for visualizing conditional frequency distributions
2.compare relative frequencies to explore the relationship between the variables
relative frequency segmented bar plot
mosaicplot
side-by-side box plots
